StarLab-CosmOdyssey Training

Saturday, January 31 9:00am-Noon

and Saturday, March 31 9:00 - Noon

Cost: $15

A planetarium is a great way to teach students about the universe around them. This certification course will teach you how to use our portable planetarium. You'll receive the instruction manual that is filled with fun and exciting activities. At the end of the workshop, sign up to rent this piece of equipment for your astronomy education program. Fee includes manual. Open to all educators.

The Nature of Science

Wednesday, February 18 5:30-8:30pm

Cost: $5

How do you get your students to write a hypothesis, test it, and draw conclusions from those tests? This workshop will walk you through the process by having you do a variety of simple activities to help you write a testable hypothesis, test it, and draw conclusions just like a scientist.

Stories in Stone

Saturday, February 21 9:00am-Noon

Cost: $5

Based on the GEMS guide, this workshop will enhance your understanding of the rock cycle. Learn how igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks are formed using real specimens. Explore the Stories in Stone teaching kit available for loan through the SRC. Recommended for 4th-8th grade teachers.

Ohio Animals

Wednesday, March 3 5:30-8:30pm

Cost: $5

Are you a K-2 teacher trying to meet the new Ohio Science Standards in Life Science? Look no further. This workshop was designed with you in mind. We'll take an in depth look at the SRC's newest thematic teaching kit, Ohio Animals. We'll investigate habitats, activities, and adaptations of native Ohio animals. Weather permitting, we'll also give you a personal tour of the new Perkins Wildlife Center where you can view lots of native Ohio wildlife.

top of page

Fantastic Fossils—Part 2

Saturday, March 13 10:00am-Noon

Cost $5

Join our Paleobotany staff for the lab portion of this series of workshops. Learn basic paleontological techniques for preparing fossils for study. You'll also gain valuable knowledge on how plant fossils are formed, where they can be found in Ohio, and how to identify specimens. This workshop was designed with the Ohio Science Life Sciences Benchmarks in mind, especially for the 4th, 8th, and 10th grade teachers. Attendance of the Part 1 workshop recommended, but not necessary.

Wildlife in African Artifacts

March 17 4:00—6:00pm

Cost: FREE

Joan Query will help teachers explore examples of animals in the S e nenkunya: Many Voices, One Family exhibit and the new ly renovated gallery of Sub-Saharan Art at the Cleveland Museum of Art. Learn how, why, and by whom these articles were made. A discussion of the objects ' intrinsic aesthetic qualities will also be included.

Getting Wet with Watersheds

Wednesday, March 31 5:30-8:30pm

Cost: $5

What is a watershed? What causes water pollution in a watershed? How are rivers, lakes, and ground water related to watersheds? These and many other questions will be answered as Barb Kooser, head naturalist at Mentor Marsh, shares with you her expertise on watersheds. Open to all educators.

Above & Out of Africa

April 21 6:30 —8 :30pm

Cost: FREE

Join Clyde Simpson & Pam Keiper on a journey across the African skies. Explore African constellations and compare traditional Greco-Roman mythology with African sky legends. Northern and southern hemisphere stars will be introduced in the new Shafran Planetarium and in the StarLab/CosmOdyssey. Sponsored in part by the Ohio Humanities Council, a state affiliate of the National Endowment for the Humanities.

top of page

Exploring African Cultures - Part I

April 28 5:30 —8 :30pm

Cost: FREE

Explore the cultures of western Africa using the Science Resource Center's newest teaching kit. Teachers (K-8) will use real artifacts to help understand time lines, history, and traditional culture. All activities and materials used during this workshop will be available for loan through the Science Resource Center. Sponsored in part by the Ohio Humanities Council, a state affiliate of the National Endowment for the Humanities.

 

Exploring African Cultures - Part 2

May 5 6:00 —8 :00pm

Cost: FREE

You'll be transported from Cleveland to modern day Africa through a variety of real objects, beautiful slides, and first person accounts. Hear first hand from a Peace Corp volunteer how modern west African culture has changed and how the people have adapted to our ever changing world. Discuss gender roles, education, and class structure as well as the traditional customs of selected societies. Sponsored in part by the Ohio Humanities Council, a state affiliate of the National Endowment for the Humanities.

Ohio Plants

Saturday, June 5 9:00am-Noon

Cost: $5

Location: Squire ValleeVue Farm

Join Bob Bartolotta, our outdoor education coordinator, at the Pink Pig on Squire ValleeVue Farm. You'll explore a wide variety of plants in 3 different habitats (wetland/pond, field, and forest) on the Farm. Learn common Ohio plant names and how to identify them. Basic plant structures and their functions will also be discussed. Directions will be provided upon registration. Please dress for the out-of-doors. Designed for the K-4 educator, but all are welcome.
