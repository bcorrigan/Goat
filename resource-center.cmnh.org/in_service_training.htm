  In-Service Training

The Science Resource Center fosters quality instruction by keeping in touch with teachers' needs through teacher in-service programs. Programs can be conducted at the Museum or at the school. They can range from 1 hour to a full day hands-on science training. The sessions are designed to teach teachers how to integrate science with language arts, math and social studies while striving to help students meet Ohio Science Standards. Teachers are also given information about SRC resources and newsletters. Programs at the Museum can accommodate up to 90 teachers. Programs at a school can accommodate up to 30 teachers for each session. Topics for the 2003-2004 school year are listed below and can be combined for full day or multi-day programs. We can also prepare customized programs. Programs held at the Museum can also include tours of the galleries, planetarium shows, and much more. Call 216-231-4600 ext. 3211 or email the SRC to schedule your school's in-service training.

The Nature of Science

How do you get your students to write a hypothesis, test it, and draw conclusions from those tests? This workshop will walk you through the process by having you do a variety of simple activities to help you write a testable hypothesis, test it, and draw conclusions just like a scientist.

Geology: The Active Earth

Hands-on geology will have teachers investigat ing volcanoes, plate tectonics, earthquakes, & the structure of the Earth. Ohio rocks & the rock cycle will be included.

Wild About Weather

What causes thunder & lightning? How do meteorologist s predict the weather? These & many other questions will be answered in this workshop. Teachers will explore the dynamics of our atmosphere and learn ex c iting hands-on activities that can be used in their classrooms.

Adventures in Astronomy

Explore the solar system, eclipses, moon phases, seasonal changes and much more. Activities that integrate math with science will be investigated. ( Ask about incorporating the certification program for StarLab with this workshop .)

Sort-it, Group-it, Classify-it

Teachers will sort, group, and classify a variety of objects, including leaves, animals, and even rocks based on structure, function, shape and other attributes. Making flow charts, Venn diagrams, and dichotomous keys will be made easy with this workshop.

top of page

Get Wild for Wildlife

Get up close and personal with a variety of Ohio wildlife. Teachers will get to see real (but not alive) animals in realistic dioramas plus get a chance to touch and feel real animal coverings. Basic needs, adaptations, and wildlife issues will be discussed. Teaching your students about Ohio's rich biodiversity has never been so “wild”. (Ask about incorporating Project WILD into this workshop.)

Water Water Everywhere

Teachers will learn safe and easy methods of experimenting with water. We will examine the physical properties, including the states of matter of water, and investigate the environmental issues associated with our waterways. Teaching students to collect, record and evaluate data will also be included.

Forever Forests

Looking at the ecology of the forest can aid students in understanding cycles and classification. With this workshop teachers will be taking an in depth look at temperate forests and tropical rainforests. Fun activities will have teachers exploring the living and nonliving characteristics of forests.

top of page

Simple Machines

Simple machines are all around us, yet this is a difficult topic for teachers and students to grasp. Teachers will become familiar with simple machines & investigate the ir advantages and basic terminology.

Customized Professional Development

Have your professional development workshops at the Museum or in your school. They can last from 1 hour to several days. Choose the topic and grade level and we'll create a workshop that fits your needs. If you choose to come to the Museum we can arrange for Behind-the-Scenes tours, Planetarium shows, tours of the Perkins Wildlife Center, and even lunch. Call (216) 231-4600 ext 3211 to discuss your staff's professional development needs.
