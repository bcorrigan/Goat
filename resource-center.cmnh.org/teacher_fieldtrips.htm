  Teacher Fieldtrips

 

Join us for the BIG DIG for TEACHERS - June, 2004

Colorado, 2003

The Nature Place in Florissant, CO was home to 14 Cleveland area teachers for one week in July, 2003. The teachers began each day at 7am with a nature hike led by experts in the natural history of the Rockies. After a hardy breakfast the group headed off to places such as Leavick Valley to see alpine glacial erosive surfaces, Mt. Sherman to hike up 14,000 feet , and the Shelf Road to walk through time.

The group would end each day with a lecture on such topics as water rights in the western US and beavers or to answer the musical question "What are we talking about?" (MOTHS!) Heated ping pong competitions and midnight swims in the pool helped to releive stress and aching muscles. Laughter and a good sense of humor helped the group learn the ecology and geology of the Rockies as well as to treat them to an experience they'll remember with a smile the rest of their lives.

The SRC plans to repeat this successful trip in July 2005. Join us!

 

BIG DIG for TEACHERS

What does an archeological experience, the shores of Lake Erie, lighthouses, boat rides, food , fun, technology and Marblehead have in common? The BIG DIG for Teachers of course! A collaborative effort between the Museum and WVIZ/PBS, the BIG DIG for teachers is scheduled for the week of June 14-18 and June 21-25, 2004

This is a chance of a lifetime. Teachers will work directly with Museum archeologists. They will train teachers in the basic field methods of archeology then let you loose at a real archeological dig site where you'll find lots of artifacts that will become part of the Musuem's collections. There will also be a special evening presenation by one of the Museum educators on the ancient peoples of Ohio.

Besides hands on excavation and artifact identification, teachers will be able to explore the rich history of the shores of Lake Erie. Travel to light houses, enjoy evening cook outs, and more. You'll also be given a chance to integrate technology with WVIZ/PBS's digital cameras. After the BIG DIG, teachers can then design their own souvenir to remember the week. Power Point and scanners will be used in the Lab at WVIZ/PBS. Teachers can then share these with their students.

Cost: $475/person for 1 week (includes field school, training & hands-on presentations by experts, 5 lunches (M-F), 4 dinners (M-TH), bottled water at dig site, snacks, tours, boat rides, and Museum fees)

College Credit: 1-3 credits will be available through Ashland University for an additional fee

** Hotels accomodations must be made by individuals.
